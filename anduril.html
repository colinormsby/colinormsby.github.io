01

Business Segments

Autonomous Airpower

Anduril's fastest-scaling segment. Develops and manufactures next-generation unmanned aircraft including the Fury CCA, Roadrunner-M interceptor, ALTIUS loitering munitions, and Ghost ISR platforms. Arsenal-1 in Columbus, Ohio — a 5M sq ft factory — is producing Fury today, targeting automotive-scale production of thousands of units annually at full ramp. The YFQ-44A "Fury" made its maiden flight October 2025 and is in active competition with General Atomics for the USAF CCA program.

Lattice Platform + CUAS

Lattice OS is the crown jewel — an AI command-and-control platform fusing thousands of sensor streams into a real-time 3D battlespace picture. The U.S. Army's $20B enterprise contract consolidates 120+ procurement actions under a single Anduril relationship. Counter-UAS includes Pulsar EW systems, Anvil interceptors, and Sentry towers deployed along the U.S. southern border ($2B IDIQ, ~$818M obligated). In February 2025, Anduril assumed Microsoft's $22B IVAS AR/VR headset program for the Army — up to 121,000 units pending combat test approval.

Heavy R&D + Arsenal-1 capex

Anduril self-funds R&D upfront — no cost-plus contracts. All dev spend hits the income statement before revenue recognition on large multi-year contracts. Arsenal-1 ($1B+ campus) represents significant pre-production capex. Losses are strategic — the finance team's job is maintaining EAC/ETC discipline on exactly these structures.

Key awards: $20B Army Lattice (10yr), $22B IVAS, $2B border IDIQ (~$818M obligated), $642M USMC cUAS, $250M Roadrunner-M DoD production.

Business Model

Unlike traditional primes who collect cost-plus margins, Anduril self-funds development and sells finished products. This commercial model compresses timelines from years to months and creates recurring upgrade revenue via Lattice OS — essentially defense SaaS bundled with hardware.

Capital Structure

Privately held. ~$6.9B across 14 rounds. Key investors: Founders Fund ($1B Series G), a16z, General Catalyst, Fidelity, Sands Capital, Baillie Gifford. Current $4B round at $60B led by a16z + Thrive. IPO expected 2026–2027.

Investment Thesis

Bull Thesis
  • Arsenal-1 as Manufacturing MoatNo other defense tech startup has committed $1B+ to a 5M sq ft autonomous weapons factory. Production scale creates cost advantages 5–10 years ahead of any VC-backed competitor.
  • Lattice as Platform Lock-inThe Army's $20B enterprise contract consolidates 120+ procurement actions under one Anduril relationship — the definition of platform stickiness.
  • Revenue Inflection with No Ceiling$236M → $500M → $1B → $2.1B in four years. $20B Army + $22B IVAS provide years of highly visible revenue.
  • Structural Geopolitical TailwindPentagon Replicator, China threat, NATO 2%+ commitments create multi-decade demand for exactly what Anduril builds.
Bear Thesis
  • Deep and Growing Losses>$800M net loss on ~$1B revenue is −80%+ operating margin. Path to profitability requires Arsenal-1 to produce at volume — execution risk is enormous.
  • IVAS is a Historically Cursed ProgramMicrosoft struggled with IVAS for years before losing it. Delivering 121K combat-ready AR headsets is a deeply difficult engineering challenge.
  • Concentrated Contract RiskA handful of mega-contracts dominate revenue. Loss or delay of Army, IVAS, or USMC awards could materially impair the growth story.
  • $60B Not Cheap at 14× 2026EPrices in aggressive execution with zero margin for error. DOGE-style budget compression would reprice sharply.

Risk Matrix

Risk FactorSeverityDescription
Arsenal-1 Execution
HIGH
Scaling a greenfield 5M sq ft weapons factory from zero is a once-in-a-generation manufacturing challenge. Production delays for Fury, Roadrunner, and Barracuda directly impair 2026 revenue guidance of $4.3B.
IVAS Combat Test Failure
HIGH
The Army must pass IVAS through high-stress operational combat tests before authorizing full-rate production of up to 121,000 units. Test failure triggers program restructuring and material revenue delay.
Defense Budget / DOGE
HIGH
Political efforts to cut federal spending could impair DoD procurement. The $20B Army and $22B IVAS programs could face reduced annual appropriations, slowing draw-down rates against contract ceilings.
CCA Competition Loss
MED
Fury (YFQ-44A) competes against General Atomics YFQ-42A for the USAF CCA program. A loss removes a major revenue catalyst but leaves the broader Lattice platform and other contract vehicles intact.
Burn Rate / Liquidity
MED
$800M+ annual losses + Arsenal-1 capex = rapid capital consumption. Relies on continued high-valuation private fundraising. A market turn could force dilutive raises at lower marks.
Geopolitical De-Escalation
MED
Diplomatic resolution in Ukraine, Taiwan Strait, or Middle East could reduce urgency around autonomous defense procurement — compressing multiples and delaying contract timelines.
Key Person / Founder Risk
LOW
Palmer Luckey's public profile and Washington relationships are brand assets. His departure would create reputational overhang, though management depth is substantial.
